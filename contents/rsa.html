Algorithm

RSA encryption consists 3 algorithm: Key generation, Encryption, Decryption

Key generation

This coloerd variables are defined by users.

This coloerd variables cannot be defined by users.

  1. Let \(\color{cyan}{p}\) and \(\color{cyan}{q}\) are big enough different prime numbers.
  2. \(\color{yellow}{\lambda(n)} = (\color{cyan}{p}-1)(\color{cyan}{q}-1)\)
  3. Let public exponent \(\color{cyan}{e}\) be a number satisfying \(\gcd(\color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}, \color{cyan}{e})\) and \(1\lt \color{cyan}{e} \lt \color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}\).
    There exist \(\color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}\) of \(\color{cyan}{e}\)'s. (\(\because\) Euler's totient function.)
    65537 is commonly used for \(\color{cyan}{e}\).
  4. These \(\color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}\) and \(\color{cyan}{e}\) are public key.
  5. The secret key \(\color{yellow}{d}\) is the number satisfying \(1\lt \color{yellow}{d} \lt \color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}\) and \(\color{cyan}{e}\times\color{yellow}{d}=1\mod\color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}\)
    The second equation is also denoted as \(\color{yellow}{d} = \color{cyan}{e}^{-1}\mod\color{yellow}{\lambda(n)}\).
Note : Modular multiplicative inverse

Definition
\(x^{-1} \equiv y \mod n \Leftrightarrow xy \equiv 1 \mod n\)

For example
\( \begin{align} 3\times 4 &=12\equiv 1 &&\mod 11\\ 3^{-1} &\equiv 4 &&\mod 11\\ 4^{-1} &\equiv 3 &&\mod 11\\ \end{align} \)

Encryption

  1. Let \(\color{red}{m}\) is the messeage to be encrypted.
  2. Calculate cryptogram \(\color{green}{c}\) as \(\color{red}{m}^\color{red}{e}\mod \color{red}{\lambda(n)}\)
